Privacy-first · Last updated April 2026

Privacy, taken seriously.

We're a small, senior team handling sensitive founder information every day. This policy tells you exactly what we collect, why we collect it, and how you stay in control of it.

01

Who controls your data

Syndavo LLC is the data controller for personal information collected through this website and during engagements. We serve clients internationally and apply the stricter of local law or the standard set out here — whichever protects you more. Where the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies to an engagement (for example, when we process personal data of EU residents), we comply with it in full.

Data protection questions? Email hello@syndavo.com with the subject line "Privacy request". We respond within two business days.

02

What we collect

Information you give us directly:

  • Name, email, company, and role when you book a discovery meeting or submit a contact form
  • Project details, business context, and any materials you share during an engagement
  • Billing information — processed by our payment provider, never stored on our servers

Information collected automatically:

  • Standard web analytics — pages visited, time on site, approximate location, device type
  • Essential cookies for site functionality (see our cookie policy)

We do not collect sensitive categories of data (racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, health data) unless explicitly necessary for an engagement and with your consent.

03

Why we collect it

Every piece of data we collect maps to one of four legal bases under GDPR:

  • Contract performance — delivering the services you hired us for
  • Legitimate interest — understanding how the site performs, improving our work, and responding to inbound inquiries
  • Consent — non-essential cookies, analytics, and any marketing email (always opt-in, never pre-ticked)
  • Legal obligation — tax, accounting, and regulatory requirements

We never use your data to train AI models, sell to data brokers, or share with advertisers. Period.

04

Who we share it with

We share personal data only with service providers that help us run the business, each under a Data Processing Agreement:

  • Google Workspace — email, documents, meeting scheduling
  • Notion — project documentation shared with clients
  • Stripe — payment processing (they never share card data back to us)
  • Cloudflare — site hosting and DDoS protection
  • Plausible Analytics — privacy-first web analytics (no cookies, no personal identifiers)

Because we operate internationally, personal data may be processed in the United States or other countries where our vendors are based. For transfers involving EU or UK personal data, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent safeguards recognised under applicable law. We select vendors with strong security and compliance postures, and will match specific cross-border data-transfer requirements documented in your SOW.

05

How long we keep it

  • Contact inquiries that don't convert — deleted after 18 months
  • Active client data — retained for the duration of the engagement plus three years
  • Invoices & financial records — seven years, per applicable tax and accounting law
  • Analytics data — aggregated, retained for 24 months

When retention periods expire, data is permanently deleted or fully anonymised.

06

Your rights

Under GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Delete your data ("right to be forgotten"), subject to legal retention requirements
  • Restrict or object to specific processing activities
  • Port your data to another provider in a machine-readable format
  • Withdraw consent at any time, with no impact on past processing

Email hello@syndavo.com to exercise any of these rights. No forms. No friction. We'll verify your identity and fulfil the request within 30 days.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the data protection or privacy regulator in your country of residence — for EU residents, your national supervisory authority; for UK residents, the Information Commissioner's Office; for other jurisdictions, the equivalent body.

07

How we protect it

We apply reasonable technical and organisational safeguards: encryption in transit (TLS 1.3), encryption at rest on all client data, multi-factor authentication on every system that holds personal data, and least-privilege access controls internally.

In the unlikely event of a breach that puts your data at risk, we'll notify affected users and the supervisory authority within 72 hours, as GDPR requires.

08

Changes & contact

We review this policy annually and update it when our practices or the law changes materially. Updates are posted here with a revised date. For questions, corrections, or formal requests: hello@syndavo.com.
