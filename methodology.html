Our process

How we work with you.

No big decks, no endless discovery. A discovery meeting, a clear plan, short sprints, and a real launch — with everything handed back to you at the end.

Typical timeline
8–12 weeks

From first meeting to launched venture, for most early-stage builds. No stretched retainers.

Clarity, not chaos.

You always know where we are, what's next, and why. No vague status updates, no mystery invoices, no “it's nearly done.”

Short loops over long plans.

We ship in weeks, not quarters. Every sprint produces something you can see, test, and change course on — before it's too expensive to.

You own what we build.

Code, accounts, docs, decisions log — all handed over at launch. No lock-in, no “re-engagement fee” to touch your own system.

The four phases

From idea to launched venture.

01

Discovery meeting.

A 30-minute call. We hear the idea, ask the awkward questions, and give you an honest read on whether it's worth pursuing — and whether we're the right ones to help.

You walk away with
  • A clearer sense of the problem
  • Early risks flagged, in plain English
  • A suggested next step. Yours to take or leave.
02

Model & roadmap.

Two focused weeks. We pressure-test the idea, sharpen the business model, define the market, and sequence the build into something you can actually hand to a team.

You walk away with
  • Validated business model
  • Defined market & first customer
  • Sequenced build plan
03

Build in short sprints.

Prototype the riskiest part first, then MVP in front of real users, then harden and integrate. Every sprint ends with something you can actually see. No 12-week black boxes.

04

Launch & handover.

We ship it, you own it. Everything is transferred into your accounts with documentation and a decisions log so anyone — us, another team, your own future hires — can pick up where we left off.

How we actually behave.

Principles we don't bend on, even when it would make a project easier in the short term.

Senior people, on the work.

The team you meet is the team that builds. No juniors shadow-billed as seniors, no handoff to an offshore pod after the kickoff call.

Small, focused, accountable.

One tight team, one point of contact, one calendar. No org charts to navigate, no mystery stakeholders showing up three weeks in with opinions.

Honest before polite.

If the idea won't work, we'll tell you — in the first meeting if we can see it. We'd rather lose the deal than take your money on something we don't believe in.
