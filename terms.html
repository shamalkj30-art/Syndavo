Terms of Service.

Plain English. No fine print. These terms govern how we engage with founders, operators, and companies that hire Syndavo. Read them once — they protect you as much as they protect us.

01

Who we are

Syndavo is a consulting and technology advisory firm serving founders and operators internationally. In these terms, "Syndavo", "we", "us", and "our" refer to Syndavo LLC and its affiliates. "You" means the individual or company engaging us under a Statement of Work ("SOW"), proposal, or retainer agreement.

By accepting a Syndavo proposal, signing an SOW, or paying an invoice from us, you agree to these terms.

02

Scope of engagement

Every engagement is defined by a written proposal or SOW. That document is the source of truth for deliverables, timelines, fees, and responsibilities. If it isn't written in the SOW, it isn't part of the engagement.

We deliver within 48 hours of a discovery call a clear proposal with scope, timeline, and fee. You're welcome to accept, negotiate, or walk away — no obligation either direction.

Scope changes happen. If a change is material, we'll document it in writing and adjust fees or timeline before continuing the work.

03

Fees & payment

Fees are fixed per project or billed monthly for retainers — no hidden hourly escalations. Invoices are due within 14 days of issue unless your SOW says otherwise.

Project-based engagements typically require a 40% deposit on signature, with the remainder on delivery. Retainers bill monthly in advance and can be paused or cancelled with 30 days' notice.

Late payments accrue interest at 1.5% per month on the outstanding balance. If an invoice remains unpaid past 45 days, we may pause active work until the account is current.

04

Intellectual property

Deliverables we create for you — strategy documents, go-to-market plans, roadmaps, source code, and written artefacts — become your property upon full payment. You own what you paid for.

We retain rights to our pre-existing frameworks, methodologies, internal tools, and generalised know-how. Nothing in an SOW transfers those unless explicitly stated.

We do not publicise engagements without your written permission. See Section 05 below for how we handle confidentiality, marketing references, and NDAs.

05

Confidentiality & NDA

Default stance: nothing leaves the room. No one — outside you and the Syndavo team assigned to your engagement — learns anything about your project, your idea, your financials, your pitch, your code, or your customer list unless you explicitly authorise it in writing.

This applies automatically from the first discovery call, before any paperwork is signed. We treat everything you share as confidential — business plans, financials, pitch decks, source code, customer lists, investor conversations, internal metrics, strategic reasoning, and anything else that is not already public.

We will not:

  • — Reference your company, logo, or engagement in our marketing, case studies, press, or website without your prior written consent.
  • — Share your materials with subcontractors, advisors, or third parties, except where you have approved the specific party in writing.
  • — Use your confidential information to advise a competing company — we screen every engagement against active clients to avoid conflicts.
  • — Publish, tweet, or post about your project in any form.

Mutual NDA, on request, before the first call. If you want a signed mutual non-disclosure agreement before our discovery meeting, email hello@syndavo.com with "NDA" in the subject line and we'll send one back within the same business day. If you have your own NDA template, send it — we'll sign reasonable mutual NDAs without negotiation.

This confidentiality obligation continues for five years after the engagement ends, and indefinitely for trade secrets, source code, and any material you designate as trade-secret-level.

Exceptions are narrow: we may disclose information if (a) you give written permission, (b) the information becomes public through no fault of ours, or (c) we're legally compelled by a court order — in which case we'll notify you first, where the law allows, so you can seek a protective order.

06

What we don't do

Syndavo provides strategic and technical advisory services. We are not lawyers, accountants, tax advisors, or licensed financial professionals. Our recommendations are commercial and operational, not legal or financial.

We'll flag when you need a specialist and happily introduce you to one from our network.

07

Warranties & liability

We perform our services with the skill and care expected of a professional consulting firm. We do not, however, guarantee specific business outcomes — fundraising success, user growth, or revenue — because those depend on factors outside our control.

To the maximum extent permitted by law, Syndavo's total liability for any claim arising out of an engagement is capped at the fees paid for that engagement in the six months preceding the claim.

Neither party is liable for indirect, consequential, or punitive damages, including lost profits or lost opportunities.

08

Termination

Either party can terminate a project engagement at any time with written notice. You pay for work completed up to the termination date; we deliver any work-in-progress artefacts upon final payment.

Retainers can be cancelled with 30 days' notice. No long contracts, ever.

We may end an engagement if you materially breach these terms or if continuing would require us to act unethically, unlawfully, or against the interests of a prior client.

09

Third-party tools

We use third-party platforms — Google Workspace, Notion, Linear, Figma, GitHub, Slack, and similar — to collaborate with clients. By engaging us, you accept that your project materials may be stored on those platforms under their standard terms. We choose vendors with strong security postures and will match any specific compliance requirements documented in your SOW.

10

Governing law & disputes

These terms are governed by the laws of the State of Wyoming, United States, without regard to conflict-of-law principles. Any dispute that cannot be resolved informally will be settled by binding arbitration, conducted in English, with the seat of arbitration in Cheyenne, Wyoming — or remotely by mutual agreement.

Before arbitration, both parties agree to attempt resolution through a 30-day good-faith negotiation period. Most disagreements get resolved on a call.

Because Syndavo works with clients across multiple jurisdictions, we'll accommodate reasonable requests to add a supplementary jurisdiction clause in your SOW (for example, recognising local consumer-protection law in your country).

11

Changes to these terms

We may update these terms to reflect changes in law or how we operate. Material changes will be posted here with an updated "Last updated" date. For active engagements, the terms in force on the date the SOW was signed continue to apply unless you and we agree otherwise in writing.

12

Contact

Questions, edits, or specific terms you need addressed in an SOW? Email hello@syndavo.com. We read everything and reply within one business day.
